Mushy Peas

A proper English delicacy, mushy peas take me back to the days of visiting my nan in the countryside of England This side dish pairs brilliantly with chips or a cheeky pint from the local pub!

Ingredients:

  • 9 ounces dried marrowfat peas
  • Boiling water
  • 2 teaspoons baking soda
  • 3 cups water
  • 1 teaspoon salt

Instructions

  1. Dissolve baking soda into boiling water. Place the peas into a bowl and pour boiling wateer over them so that they are covered by at least 3 inches of water. Give the peas a stir then let them soak for at least 12 hours.
  2. Drain and rinse the peas and place them in a pot with about 3 cups of water. Bring to a boil, cover, and reduce the heat to medium-low, letting them simmer for about 30 minutes while stirring occasionally. Once the desired consistency has been raeched and the peas have fully broken down, add salt.
  3. If the peas are too watery, continue to simmer with the lid off until it thickens to your liking. If the peas are too thick, add a little water. Salt to taste.
