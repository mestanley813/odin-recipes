Jellied Eels

There's nothing I love more than a bowl full of chilled eels in the morning for brekkie. This dish is a traditional British dish stemming from the 18th Century. I fancy a day when I can eat gourmet jellied eels with the Queen.

Ingredients:

  • 900 g Skinned Eels
  • Malt Vinegar
  • 4 whole Cloves
  • 3 Bay Leaves
  • 8 Black Peppercorns
  • 4 tsp Salt
  • 1 Lemon (zest and juice)
  • 1 small bunch Curly Parsley
  • Freshly ground White Pepper
  • Brown Bread and Butter

Instructions

  1. Prepare the eel by using a sharp knife to cut the skin of the eel from the back of the head. Then, using fish pliers, pull away 2.5 cm of its skin.
  2. After skinning, lay the eel on the chopping board and cut off the head. Make a cut on the backbone, just above the lines of bones.
  3. Now, cut away the fillet. Keep the knife close to the bones.
  4. When the knife is close to the bones, hold the eel from another side and cut the fillet in a clean sweep. Repeat the same for the other side.
  5. Now cut the fillet into pieces of around 2.5cm in length diagonally.
  6. Take a large saucepan and add pieces with the ingridients, i.e., lemon juice, bay leaves, peppercorn, cloves, and salt.
  7. Pour cold water into the saucepan and bring this mixture to boil for about 20 minutes.
  8. Now, put the mixture in the bowl and cool it. After 10 minutes, stir it after adding parsley and chill it until the mixture is set.
  9. After the mixture is set and the jelly is formed, it is ready to be served.
